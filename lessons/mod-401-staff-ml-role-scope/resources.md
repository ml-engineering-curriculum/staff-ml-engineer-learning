# Resources for mod-401-staff-ml-role-scope

The references below are the primary sources the chapters cite. Read the canonical texts (Larson, StaffEng.com stories) before the module; use the framework references and the industry essays as triangulation.

## Canonical texts on the staff-plus IC role

- **Will Larson.** *Staff Engineer: Leadership beyond the management track.* Stripe Press, 2021. [staffeng.com/book](https://staffeng.com/book). The four archetypes in chapter 2 come from here. Chapter 1 of the book (*Overview of the Staff Engineer Role*) is the shortest possible standalone reading for this module.
- **StaffEng.com stories corpus.** [staffeng.com/stories](https://staffeng.com/stories/). Public interviews with staff-plus engineers across Stripe, Slack, Google, Netflix, Fastly, Squarespace, and other companies. Larson's four-archetype taxonomy was distilled from this corpus.
- **Tanya Reilly.** *Being Glue* (talk + essay). [noidea.dog/glue](https://noidea.dog/glue). Names the non-promotable coordination work that keeps organisations running — background reading for how *not* to write the Staff/EM partnership contract (chapter 5).
- **Tanya Reilly.** *The Staff Engineer's Path.* O'Reilly, 2022. Companion reading to Larson; deeper treatment of the influence and communication axes chapter 6's self-assessment maps against.

## Peer-level framework references

- **Camille Fournier.** *The Manager's Path.* O'Reilly, 2017. The canonical L40 EM ladder text. Chapter 5's partnership contract inverts Fournier's L40 EM mandate to derive the Staff side.
- **Camille Fournier.** *An Incomplete List of Skills Senior Engineers Need.* [skamille.medium.com](https://skamille.medium.com/an-incomplete-list-of-skills-senior-engineers-need-beyond-coding-8ed6f3f37f6). Useful as a cross-check on the L30 verb list in chapter 3.
- **Charity Majors.** *The Engineer/Manager Pendulum* (2017). [charity.wtf/2017/05/11/the-engineer-manager-pendulum](https://charity.wtf/2017/05/11/the-engineer-manager-pendulum/). The standard essay on how careers oscillate between the IC and manager paths; important context for why chapter 5 treats the two L40 paths as equal disciplines rather than one being a promotion of the other.

## Engineering-career frameworks (triangulation for the archetypes)

- **Engineering Ladders.** [engineeringladders.com](https://www.engineeringladders.com/). Public five-axis framework (technology, system, people, process, influence). Chapter 2 cross-references Larson's four archetypes against this.
- **progression.fyi.** [progression.fyi](https://www.progression.fyi/). Aggregates public engineering-career frameworks from Meta, Rent the Runway, Monzo, Buffer, and others. Chapter 2 uses this as the "no single framework is canonical" reference.
- **LeadDev.** [leaddev.com](https://leaddev.com/). Ongoing archive of essays and conference talks on staff-plus engineering.

## ML-org-specific grounding

- **Chip Huyen.** *Designing Machine Learning Systems.* O'Reilly, 2022. Portfolio-scope architecture framing that chapter 4's hand-off contracts (peer specialist, peer platform, peer governance) lean on.
